Donning and Doffing PPE Procedures

A comprehensive guide for healthcare professionals

Assessment of Donning Procedure

Before donning Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), healthcare workers must perform a thorough assessment to ensure proper protection and adherence to infection control protocols.

Patient Condition

Assess the need for PPE based on the patient's condition and isolation protocol.

Environment

Evaluate the environment for cleanliness and availability of PPE.

Healthcare Worker

Assess the healthcare worker's knowledge and skill level related to donning.

Contraindications

Ensure absence of contraindications (e.g., allergies to latex or specific PPE materials).

PPE Type

Confirm the type of PPE required (Standard, Contact, Droplet, Airborne precautions).

Assessment Before Doffing PPE

Doffing Area

Ensure designated doffing area is available and clean.

Supplies

Check availability of hand hygiene supplies and waste bins.

Contamination

Check for visible contamination on PPE.

Staff Preparedness

Ensure staff is trained and mentally prepared to doff without rushing.

Assistant

Confirm assistant (if available) is ready to guide or monitor the procedure.

Donning and Doffing Procedures

Donning Procedure

1
Perform hand hygiene
2
Put on shoe cover
3
Put on head cap
4
Put on gown (tie at neck & waist)
5
Put on mask/respirator (fit properly)
6
Put on eye protection (goggles/face shield)
7
Put on gloves (cover cuffs of gown)
8
Done

Doffing Procedure

1
Remove shoe cover
2
Remove gloves
3
Remove face shield/goggles
4
Remove gown
5
Remove head cap
6
Remove mask/respirator
7
Discard all PPE safely
8
Final hand hygiene

Required Equipment

Alcohol-based hand rub / Soap & water
Waste disposal bin (biohazard)
Mirror (optional, for self-monitoring)
Assistant/observer checklist (optional)
Mask/Respirator
Gown
Goggles/Face shield
Gloves
Shoe covers
Head cap

Prerequisite Knowledge

Understanding Sequence

Understanding the correct sequence of PPE removal to minimize contamination risk.

Hand Hygiene

Importance of hand hygiene after every step of doffing procedure.

Self-Contamination Prevention

Proper technique to avoid self-contamination during doffing.

PPE Types

Familiarity with different types of PPE and their proper removal techniques.

Local Protocols

Knowledge of local infection control protocols and guidelines.

Contamination Awareness

Understanding which PPE surfaces are considered contaminated and how to handle them safely.
